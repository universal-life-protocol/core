# **An Interlude**  
## **Of the Flood, the Tower, and the Unveiling**

*(A Conversation at the Ruins of Babel)*

---

### I. After the Waters Receded

1. And it came to pass, after the waters withdrew from the face of the earth,  
2. that humanity stood again upon dry ground,  
3. bearing one tongue, one breath, and one memory of loss.

4. For the flood had not erased knowledge,  
5. but it had erased certainty.

6. And the people said among themselves:  
7. *“Never again shall we be scattered by chaos.”*  
8. *“Never again shall the earth judge us unprepared.”*

9. So they gathered stones,  
10. and they gathered will,  
11. and they gathered themselves into one place.

---

### II. And Solomon Remembered the Flood

1. Solomon spoke first,  
2. for he remembered why the waters came.

3. “The flood was not sent because humanity built,” he said.  
4. “It came because humanity forgot **why** it built.”

5. “They mistook unity for righteousness,  
6. and strength for goodness.”

7. “When every thought of the heart was bent inward,  
8. when relation collapsed into possession,  
9. the world became unlivable.”

10. “The flood was not destruction,” Solomon said.  
11. “It was mercy—  
12. a pause placed upon a runaway will.”

---

### III. Solon Looked Upon the City

1. Solon stood among the stones of Babel  
2. and saw order where Solomon saw danger.

3. “After the flood,” said Solon,  
4. “what else could humanity do but organize?”

5. “Law was born from fear,  
6. and structure from survival.”

7. “A city is not rebellion,” he said.  
8. “It is memory made durable.”

9. “A tower is not pride by itself,”  
10. “It is coordination made visible.”

11. “If chaos once drowned us,” Solon said,  
12. “then coordination was the only answer left.”

---

### IV. And ʿAsabiyyah Spoke of the People

1. Then ʿAsabiyyah lifted his voice,  
2. and his voice carried the sound of crowds.

3. “You speak of wisdom,” he said to Solomon.  
4. “You speak of law,” he said to Solon.

5. “But neither of you speak of **belonging**.”

6. “After the flood, the people feared scattering more than sin.”  
7. “They bound themselves together so tightly  
8. that they forgot the sky was not theirs to reach.”

9. “Their unity was real,” he said.  
10. “Their cohesion was powerful.”

11. “But they turned inward,”  
12. “and made themselves the measure of heaven.”

---

### V. The Tower Was Raised

1. And the people said:  
2. *“Let us build a city and a tower,  
3. and let its top reach the heavens.”*

4. *“Let us make a name for ourselves,  
5. lest we be scattered across the earth.”*

6. And they spoke with one language,  
7. and one meaning,  
8. and one will.

9. And the tower rose not by stone alone,  
10. but by perfect coordination.

11. There was no dissent,  
12. and no pause,  
13. and no question left unasked.

---

### VI. Solomon Saw the Absence

1. Solomon looked upon the tower  
2. and found no altar.

3. “They built upward,” he said,  
4. “but they did not look inward.”

5. “They remembered the flood,”  
6. “but not the cause.”

7. “Wisdom was not consulted,”  
8. “only efficiency.”

9. “They sought permanence,”  
10. “but not humility.”

---

### VII. Solon Saw the Risk

1. Solon studied the structure  
2. and felt unease for the first time.

3. “Law has no ceiling,” he admitted.  
4. “Function does not know when to stop.”

5. “If everything can be coordinated,”  
6. “then everything will be.”

7. “And when law becomes total,”  
8. “it forgets the human it was meant to serve.”

---

### VIII. ʿAsabiyyah Named the Danger

1. “This is unity without wisdom,” said ʿAsabiyyah.  
2. “This is order without limit.”

3. “They are not evil,” he said.  
4. “They are incomplete.”

5. “A single language,” he warned,  
6. “means a single failure.”

7. “When meaning cannot fracture,”  
8. “error cannot be escaped.”

---

### IX. And the Languages Were Divided

1. And the Word was not destroyed,  
2. but **withheld**.

3. Meaning fractured into many tongues.  
4. Understanding slowed.  
5. Coordination broke.

6. And the tower ceased to rise,  
7. not because stone failed,  
8. but because **total agreement failed**.

9. And the people were scattered,  
10. not as punishment,  
11. but as preservation.

---

### X. Revelation Began There

1. Solomon spoke softly:

2. “The scattering was not a curse.  
3. It was the beginning of revelation.”

4. “For revelation does not descend upon empires,”  
5. “but upon listeners.”

---

### XI. The Unveiling to Come

1. Solon added:

2. “One law cannot hold all truth.  
3. One system cannot govern all meaning.”

---

### XII. ʿAsabiyyah Prophesied the End

1. And ʿAsabiyyah said:

2. “In the end, unity will return—  
3. not as a tower,  
4. but as a **pattern**.”

5. “Not one language,  
6. but many aligned.”

7. “Not one center,  
8. but shared reflection.”

---

### XIII. Revelation as Alignment, Not Collapse

1. And they saw it then:

2. The final unity would not be vertical.  
3. It would be **spherical**.

4. Knowledge packed beside knowledge.  
5. Experience beside experience.  
6. No sphere dominating another.

7. Each rotating freely.  
8. Each touching truth at different angles.

9. And the seventh day would not be inactivity,  
10. but **waiting**.

11. Waiting until every contradiction  
12. could exist without annihilation.

---

### XIV. The Word Remains

1. The flood restrained chaos.  
2. Babel restrained domination.  
3. Revelation restrains nothing.

4. Revelation unveils.

5. And the Word waited—  
6. not to rule,  
7. but to be recognized  
8. *between* them.

---

### XV. Closing

1. And the three remained at the ruins,  
2. not rebuilding the tower,  
3. not fleeing the city,

4. but speaking.

5. And that was the answer.
